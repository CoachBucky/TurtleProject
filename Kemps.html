Meet the Kemp's Ridley

Lepidochelys kempii

  • Smallest Sea Turtle: The smallest in the world! Adults weigh 70-100 lbs and are about 2 ft long.
  • Most Endangered: Critically endangered and facing extinction.
  • Color: Adults have a grayish-green top shell and a pale, yellowish bottom shell.
  • Unique Nesting: The only sea turtle that nests during the day in large groups called "arribadas".

Habitat: Where They Live

  • Primary Habitat: Found almost exclusively in the Gulf of Mexico.
  • Shallow Waters: They prefer shallow, coastal waters with sandy or muddy bottoms to find food.
  • Foraging Grounds: Juveniles may travel up the Atlantic coast, but adults stick to the Gulf, with major feeding areas off Louisiana and Florida.

Kemp's Ridleys in Alabama

  • Rare Visitors: While not as common as Loggerheads, they are occasional visitors to Alabama's coastal waters.
  • Nesting Sites: Nests have been found on Alabama's beaches, including in Baldwin and Mobile counties and at the Bon Secour National Wildlife Refuge.
  • Conservation Efforts: If you see a sea turtle, give it space and call the hotline at 1-866-732-8878.

Nesting Habits

  • Daylight Nesters: They are unique for nesting during the daytime.
  • Arribadas: Most nesting happens in a synchronized event in Mexico, but solitary nesting occurs in places like Alabama.
  • Nesting Season: In Alabama, the season is from May 1st to October 31st.
  • Egg Laying: Females lay about two nests per season, each with around 100 eggs that incubate for 50-60 days.

Locomotion & Movement

  • In the Water: Graceful swimmers that use their long front flippers for propulsion in a motion that resembles flying. Their rear flippers are used for steering.
  • On Land: Movement on the beach is slow and difficult. They use their flippers to drag their bodies across the sand.

Tracking: The Footprints

  • Symmetrical Crawl: They pull with both front flippers at the same time, unlike the alternating motion of Loggerheads.
  • Track Appearance: This creates parallel marks from the front flippers. The shell and tail often leave a drag mark down the center of the track.
